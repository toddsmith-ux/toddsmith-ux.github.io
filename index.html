Journey into UX

My journey into UX started in 2014. I studied technical writing at Clemson University from 2014 to 2016. After completing the Master’s degree, I started my first job as a technical writer. It was an incredibly enlightening experience and I gained insight about working in the STEM world. I wrote help documentation for an electrical engineering company. Our team worked diligently to ensure that the information architecture in our help documentation was easy to follow.

As time progressed, I observed that we did not have any direct interaction with our users to test whether or not our help documentation was useful. Consequently, I realized that my career goals involve understanding how users use a product when the designer is not present. Thus, in the fall of 2018, I started the UNC Chapel Hill Master of Science in Information Science program. My focus is on Human Computer Interaction. I finish the program in May 2020.

My Research and Design Philosophies

There are two main factors that guide my design philosophy: strategic planning and data driven decisions.

Data Driven Decisions

One of the founding principles of UX is that designers are not their users. Thus, designers must ensure that they want to base their design decisions around user needs. However, I believe that designers should collect sufficient data to prove that your users actually want a feature or product. Data provides design justifications.

Strategic Planning

In my experience with UX, I’ve found that you can easily run tests without considering how they relate to your overall goals. Thus, I believe that teams should practice strategic planning when conducting tests. At the beginning of the year, UX teams should identify their goals for the year and analyze the consistency with the overall goals. Afterwards, the UX team should use these goals to plan accordingly. Ultimately, I believe that strategic planning will establish a solid foundation for UX.
