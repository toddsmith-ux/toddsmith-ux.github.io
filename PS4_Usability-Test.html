Usability Test

To identify current problems within the PS4 app, I hired testers from UserTesting to test the current app. For this test, I set up a draft account so that the testers could experience the full process of logging in. Overall, I had three different testers complete ten different tasks. The tasks include a wide range of interactions with the PS4 app, ranging from logging out of the app to adding a friend in the PS4 app.

Analysis

From the testersâ€™ feedback, I identified the following problems with the current PS4 app:

1. Downloading a separate app for messaging is confusing.

2. Unclear language about denying a friend request.

3. Unclear path to log out feature.

Recommendations

Thus, to address these issues, I planned to implement the following features:

1. Create a Messaging feature within the app.

2. Change language to clarify actions around friend requests.

3. Move log out feature to profile.

Goals and Metrics

My goal for this test was to identify potential user problems within the UI. My testers did not specifically provide feedback about slow updates for games. However, I did not ask them to complete a task to download a game because that could take hours. I wanted the tests to be brief. Thus, I considered the update problem out of the scope for this test.

Although I did not conduct a follow up usability test due to time constraints, I identified two metrics for success for the app. First, I will measure user satisfaction with messaging. Second, I will measure the amount of time for users to log out of the app.
